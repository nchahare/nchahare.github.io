As someone who has lived abroad for several years, I've become intimately familiar with the joys and challenges of navigating the visa system. Recently, however, my experience with visa renewal has been particularly frustrating and anxiety-inducing.

I have a Spanish residence permit that was valid until the end of 2022. In mid-November, I applied for a renewal, but the application was rejected because of errors in the documents provided by my HR department. We reapplied in mid-December, but didn't hear anything back. January came and went, and I began to feel increasingly nervous. I had a conference in Germany and two weddings to attend in India, but I couldn't travel because of my visa situation. While technically I could have traveled to India with a "regreso" document, I chose to skip the weddings due to the high cost and uncertainty involved. To make matters worse, I also had dental issues that required costly treatment.

When I went to the police station to obtain the "regreso" document, I was told that it could only be used for immigration outside the Schengen area, and not to travel to Germany. I was left without any document to prove my legal status, and I was worried that the airline might refuse to let me board the plane when they saw my expired visa.

But my experience with visa renewal goes beyond just bureaucratic hurdles. It's important to recognize that the visa system and border control policies are not merely obstacles, but are part of a broader system of power and inequality. The wealthy and privileged are able to navigate the system with relative ease, while those from less privileged backgrounds are often denied the same opportunities due to the high cost and complexity of visa applications. This creates an unjust global hierarchy where some are able to move freely and pursue better opportunities, while others are forced to remain in their countries of origin under oppressive conditions.

Moreover, border control policies are often used to justify xenophobic and racist attitudes, perpetuating the idea that some people are more deserving of rights and opportunities than others based on their nationality or ethnicity. This divisive and unequal society is unacceptable, and we must recognize that our current immigration policies are not serving the interests of the vast majority of people, but rather, are upholding the interests of the wealthy and powerful.

Therefore, it is imperative that we push for more humane and equitable immigration policies that prioritize human rights and dignity for all, regardless of their background or economic status. We must resist the xenophobic and racist narratives that seek to divide us, and instead work towards building a world that is based on compassion, solidarity, and justice for all.

In the end, my visa was eventually renewed, but the experience left me with a renewed sense of frustration and anger at the injustices inherent in our immigration system. I hope that by sharing my story, we can continue to raise awareness about the need for more just and equitable immigration policies, and work towards a world that is more welcoming and inclusive for all.
