## Introduction

Organic life is full of folds. Our bodies are folded and curved. Our organs like lungs and intestines are too. Molecular and biological sciences have revealed geometric folds all the way through the length scales from tissues and cells to protein molecules. One of the ways scientists have visualized all these beautiful structures is origami. Recent physics have shown that many of these folds are emergent properties where tissues or proteins are optimizing energy by conforming to a particular structure. Here we will showcase the length scale and complexity of the organic matter.

---

## Methods

The art installation will be 16 paper objects. The eight will be 3D origami structures and other eight will be same origami structures but as a flat (2D) sheet attached on the wall. The flat sheets will be crumpled and marked indicating previously made folds or cuts. They will also have a short picture or description of the real organic object. The paper used for making these origamis will range from 841x841 mm (square cut out of A0) to 74x74 mm (square cut out of A7). We will organize the 2D sheets in a row with increasing size. And hang the origami structure in front of the corresponding sheet.

### Shape and size of things

1. Hydrogen atom: $100pm = 1\times10^{-7}mm$ : **No folds**
2. Aminoacids and proteins: $1nm = 1\times10^{-6}mm$ : **Molecular origami** (ref2)
3. DNA helix: $10nm = 1\times10^{-5}mm$ : **Double helix** (ref3)
4. Nucleopore complex: $125nm = 1.25\times10^{-4}mm$ : **Flasher** (ref1 Fig 10)
5. Actin bundles: $1\mu m = 1\times10^{-3}mm$ : **network or helixes**
5. Cell: $15\mu m = 1.5\times10^{-2}mm$ : **Le crimp** (ref4 page 8)
6. Blastocyst: $140\mu m = 1.4\times10^{-1}mm$ : **Origami Buckyball** (ref4 page 10, ref5)
7. Intestinal tissue: $1mm$ : **Miura ori** (ref6)
